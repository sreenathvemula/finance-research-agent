Ethical Investment:
The Act Itself Must Be Morally Neutral or Good
The Good Effect Must Be Intended, Not the Bad
The Good Effect Cannot Flow from the Bad Effect
Proportionality: The Good Must Outweigh the Bad

Practical Decision-Making Framework
Primary Moral Orientation: Does this action/investment primarily serve or harm the most vulnerable?
Trajectory Assessment: Is the entity moving toward or away from justice?
Alternative Analysis: Are there ways to achieve the good without participating in the harm?
Witness Consideration: What does my participation communicate about my values?

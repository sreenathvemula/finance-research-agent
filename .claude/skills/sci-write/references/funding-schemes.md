# Funding schemes — structures, scoring, tactics, pitfalls

Per-scheme playbooks for the four target applications. Links verified July 2026. Deadlines from
the user's comparison table + web verification; always reconfirm on the official call page before
planning backwards from a date.

---

## 1. MSCA Postdoctoral Fellowship (ENV panel)

**Facts:** deadline ~9 Sep 2026 (call opened Apr 2026); results Feb 2027, start ~mid-2027;
eligibility ≤8 yrs post-PhD, ≤12 months in Germany in last 36; 12–24 months; salaried position
(~€6,060/mo living + €710 mobility + €660 family allowances). Success rate ~10%; funding line
sits around the 92nd percentile, so a single weak section is fatal.
Official: https://marie-sklodowska-curie-actions.ec.europa.eu/actions/how-to-apply ·
https://rea.ec.europa.eu/funding-and-grants/horizon-europe-marie-sklodowska-curie-actions/msca-postdoctoral-fellowships_en

**What you submit:** Part A (admin, in portal) + **Part B1, hard limit 10 A4 pages** (the scored
document) + Part B2 (CV, host capacities, ethics; not scored by page).

**Scoring: Excellence 50% · Impact 30% · Implementation 20%** (each 0–5, half points).
Page split that works: Excellence ~6–6.5 pp, Impact ~2 pp, Implementation ~1.5 pp.

**Part B1 section map (write to the template bullets — evaluators score against them):**
- **1.1 Quality/innovation/credibility of research**: state of the art → gap → vision →
  3–5 specific objectives (output-verbs: "quantify", "characterize", not "explore") → methodology
  → explicitly "beyond the state of the art" (a comparison table of existing vs. proposed works well).
- **1.2 Two-way transfer of knowledge**: itemize BOTH directions — fellow→host (methods, data,
  networks you bring) and host→fellow (skills, infrastructure, supervision you gain). One-directional
  transfer is a named rejection cause.
- **1.3 Supervision/hosting**: supervisor track record, host infrastructure — quantified
  (for GFZ: MSCA/ERC track record, networks, data holdings, compute).
- **1.4 Researcher capacity**: career trajectory toward independence; name the next step
  (e.g., "ERC Starting Grant on X from position Y").
- **2.1 Impact on career + society**: credible route, tied to EU priorities (Green Deal, disaster
  resilience) — not "will revolutionize".
- **2.2 Dissemination/Communication/Exploitation**: three separate channels, each with
  quantified KPIs (N papers in named venues, datasets/code releases, policy briefs, outreach counts).
- **3.1 Work plan**: WPs mapped 1:1 to objectives, Gantt chart (essential), deliverables vs.
  milestones distinguished; include non-technical WPs (training, dissemination, management).
- **3.2 Risks**: real risks with mitigations. "No risks" reads as non-credible.

**Known rejection causes:** unbalanced proposal (all Excellence, thin Impact/Implementation);
ignoring template bullets; vague DEC without KPIs; overclaimed impact; missing risk table;
one-way knowledge transfer; unjustified host choice; gap not shown to matter *now*; overpacked
2-year work plan; no career-development narrative; recycled generic methodology; skipped ethics.

**Practical:** ~7 of the 10 pages go to criteria-servicing content, ~3 to the raw idea — budget
accordingly (Nowosad). German NCP **KoWi offers free proposal proofreading** for German-host
applicants: msca-proposal-check@kowi.de (https://www.kowi.de/en/kowi/news/proofreading-of-msca-postdoctoral-fellowship-proposals.aspx).
Current-cycle handbook: RADIANCE PF Handbook 2026
(https://horizoneuropencpportal.eu/sites/default/files/2026-06/radiance_handbook_postdoctoral_fellowships_2026.pdf).
Peer-reviewed checklist: "Ten simple rules for a successful MSCA PF application"
(https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC9387847/).

## 2. DFG Eigene Stelle (Research Grant with Temporary Position for the PI)

**Facts:** rolling submission (no deadline), decision ~6–9 months; doctorate required, no time
cap; up to 3 years, renewable up to 3 more; pays your own postdoc-level salary (module
"Temporary Positions for PIs", Form 52.02) plus project funds (Basic Module). Individual-grant
success ~30%. Submission only via elan portal. Proposals in German or English.
Master index: https://www.dfg.de/en/research-funding/funding-opportunities/programmes/individual/research-grants/forms-guidelines

**Critical structural point for a GFZ host:** GFZ is a Helmholtz centre, and the "duty to
cooperate" rules (Form 55.01) mean an Eigene Stelle there normally requires a **joint project
with a university partner** — waived if you are within 6 years of your doctorate AND the
institute covers 45% of total costs. Settle this with GFZ's grants office before writing a word.
(Form 52.02; FAQ: https://www.dfg.de/en/research-funding/proposal-funding-process/faq/temporary-positions)

**Hard format rules (Form 54.01, 06/26):** project description ≤ **25 pages** (≤17 pp for
sections 1–3, ≤8 pp from section 4); **Arial ≥11 pt, line spacing ≥1.2** (publication list may
be 9 pt); keep the template (Form 53.01) formatting; PDFs ≤10 MB. Generative-AI use in drafting
**must be disclosed** "in a scientifically appropriate manner."

**Project description structure (template 53.01), with the reviewer question each section answers
(Form 10.206 — reviewers judge ONLY the proposal text, they need not read your citations):**
1. **State of the art & preliminary work** — concise, complete, understandable without outside
   reading; show exactly where your "unique, innovative, promising contribution" lands.
   *Reviewer Q1: originality and contribution to knowledge; Q3: soundness of preliminary work +
   "is the applicant qualified to independently lead this project?"*
2. **Objectives and work programme** — 2.1 duration; 2.2 objectives (explicitly
   hypothesis-driven — DFG says formulate hypotheses/clear objectives, and results beyond science
   for policy/economy/society); 2.3 **work programme + methods per applicant** — "the quality of
   the work programme is critical to the success of a funding proposal"; schedule of planned
   work, what exists vs. what needs developing, alternative strategies for risky parts;
   2.4 **research-data handling** (explicitly scored — for waveform/ML work: datasets, FAIR
   repositories, code/model releases; follow www.dfg.de/research_data/checklist); 2.5 sex/gender/
   diversity relevance (brief statement if not relevant).
   *Reviewer Q2: clear working hypotheses, suitability of methods, appropriateness of schedule,
   research-data handling.*
3. **Project-related publication list** — only works cited above; max 10 own works highlighted;
   nothing non-public.
4. **Supplementary info** — ethics/legal (note 4.1.5.1 dual-use/export for AI work), employment
   status, first-proposal flag, cooperations + conflicts, equipment/compute, other submissions.
5. **Requested modules/funds** — every item traceable to the work programme (untraceable budget
   is a named DFG weakness).
Attachments: CV on template 53.200 (Category A: ≤10 peer-reviewed; Category B: ≤10 other outputs —
datasets, software, model releases count) + employer's declaration (Form 41.027) from the German host.

**DFG's own writing tips** (https://www.dfg.de/en/research-funding/proposal-funding-process/individual-grants-programmes/tips-proposals):
missing citations are noticed immediately; hypotheses double as the work-programme outline;
consider the full spectrum of contemporary methods, not just those you know; pair high-risk
approaches with lower-risk components; DFG funds **new** ideas — preliminary work proves
capability, it cannot be the project. A funded ecology proposal (Joschinski 2016,
https://doi.org/10.3897/rio.2.e9580) carries an explicit "Adaptations" subsection — risk
management baked into the methods.

**Pitfalls:** Helmholtz cooperation rule ignored; treating it as a fellowship (it's a project
grant — the project is scored as hard as you are); page/font violations; vague research-data
section; budget not derived from work programme; missing employer declaration or
leave-of-absence proof; >10 highlighted publications; undisclosed AI drafting.

## 3. Humboldt Research Fellowship (postdoc line)

**Facts:** rolling submission, selection committee meets **March / July / November** — submit
4–7 months before a target meeting; calls cap at 800 applications per round and close when full.
Decision on documents alone, **no interview**. Approval ~20–25%. €3,000/mo stipend + allowances
(+€340 spouse, +€270/child), 6–24 months (+12 months extension possible with children), optional
2–4 month German course; host receives €800/mo (STEM) research allowance.
Programme page: https://www.humboldt-foundation.de/en/apply/sponsorship-programmes/humboldt-research-fellowship ·
Programme info PDF (Jan 2026): https://www.humboldt-foundation.de/fileadmin/Bewerben/Programme/Humboldt-Forschungsstipendium/humboldt-fellowship_programme_information.pdf

**Eligibility gates (check before writing):** PhD ≤4 years ago (postdoc line); ≤90 days in
Germany in the 18 months before applying (any purpose — prior GFZ visits count); no doctoral
project done in Germany; publications commensurate with career stage; English suffices for
natural sciences.

**What you submit:** CV (**2 pages max**); **research outline ≤5 pages including bibliography**;
complete publication list + key-publications list (1–3, PDFs uploaded); doctoral certificate;
**two referee reviews** (for postdocs one should normally be the doctoral supervisor; ≤12 months
old; uploaded directly by referees); host mentoring agreement + confidential host statement
(uploaded by host after you submit). Incomplete applications are excluded — you own completeness.

**The outline must answer, in this order (from the Foundation's own host page):**
1. Current state of research — evidenced by ~5 relevant publications, **one page max**;
2. What questions are to be examined;
3. Significance of those questions for the advancement of the research area;
4. Scientific methods to achieve the objectives, substantiated by references.

**Selection criteria:** academic record (mobility, determination, productivity); quality of the
named key publications (originality + your contribution); **originality and innovation potential
of the outline** (significance for the discipline, convincing methods, feasibility within the
period at that host); future potential. Judged **relative to career level**. The idea must be
demonstrably **yours** — a proposal reading as the host's project is penalised; the host
statement separately vouches for *your* creative potential.

**Tactics from a real funded proposal** (Graziotin 2016, full text:
https://doi.org/10.3897/rio.2.e8865): tight abstract stating gap + one broad research question +
exactly three sub-questions + concrete deliverables; state-of-the-art written as a funnel ending
in one explicit gap sentence; preliminary work framed honestly when pivoting fields ("I have
never worked on this specific topic, but…") with each prior paper cited as proof of a specific
method capability; four short titled objectives, each mapping to a work package with method,
sample, partner, and month, anchored to one timeline figure; host fit argued through the science
(the host's tools and groups appear inside the methodology); explicit ethics note. A fellow's
firsthand account (https://indiabioscience.org/columns/stories-from-scientists/from-phd-to-postdoc-my-path-to-the-humboldt-fellowship):
first paragraphs must state the objective, why it matters, and how it differs from existing
work; figures with captions help; secure an enthusiastic host early (it takes months); pick
referees who genuinely know your work.

**Pitfalls:** the 90-day and 4-year gates; outline >5 pages or state-of-research >1 page
(bibliography counts inside the 5); idea reading as the host's; stale or wrong referees;
applying late into a filling round; generic "leading institution" host-fit lines.

## 4. GFZ Discovery Fellowship

**Facts:** up to **2 fellowships/year, 3 years each**, for "highly innovative/high-risk"
projects; current (8th) call deadline **Sunday 13 September 2026, noon**, for fellowships
2027–2029; pipeline is written application → shortlist → presentations (~6 weeks later, 2025
round presented 20 Oct) → start the following January. TVöD salaried position.
Programme page: https://www.gfz.de/en/research/young-scientists/gfz-discovery-fellowship-programme ·
Application platform + call text: https://www.geo-x.net/gfz-discovery-fellowship/start ·
Contact: gfzdiscoveryfellow-application@gfz.de (Franziska Alberg).

**Eligibility:** PhD in geosciences or related field **<6 years** ago (parental-leave
extensions); internal or external candidates; ≥1 peer-reviewed publication; internal candidates
cannot host in their previous supervising section.

**Project requirements (the two structural gates):**
1. Topic must map to at least one **PoF IV Program Topic** (eligible: 1, 2, 3, 5, 8 or MESI;
   seismology's home is "Restless Earth"). Topic descriptions:
   https://www.geo-x.net/fileadmin/gfzdiscoveryfellow/GFZ_Discovery_Fellowship_POF_IV_Topic_descriptions.pdf
2. **Interdisciplinarity is mandatory: ≥2 GFZ sections** — one leading (hosting) + ≥1
   supporting, with Section Head Statements from each. **Each section may put forward only ONE
   candidate as leading section per call** — you must win the internal section nomination before
   the external competition starts. Approach section heads months before the deadline.

**What you submit:** research proposal (the core — judged on **innovation and
interdisciplinarity**), motivation letter, CV, section-head statements, via the geo-x.net portal.

**What wins (pattern from past fellows):** projects that apply a method from one discipline to a
second discipline's problem — 2026: Münchmeyer, deep learning + distributed acoustic sensing for
micro-earthquake location in subduction zones (Sections 4.1 Lithosphere Dynamics + 2.4 Seismology
+ 1.1 Geodesy); Sobhkhizmiandehi, ionospheric trends under climate change (1.1 + 2.3). 2024:
Steinmann, seismic sensing for wildlife ecology; Täumer, soil methane microbiome. Earlier: Zwaan
(rifting → natural hydrogen), Shevchenko (volcano–cryosphere via UAV photogrammetry).
An ML-methods-bridging-two-geoscience-sections construction has a direct funded precedent.
(https://www.gfz.de/en/press/news/details/gfz-discovery-fellowship-2026-awarded-to-s-sobhkhizmiandehi-and-j-muenchmeyer)

**Pitfalls:** treating the ≥2-sections requirement as a checkbox rather than the design
principle; missing the internal nomination window; proposing incremental (not "high-risk
innovative") work — the fund exists for discovery-class ideas; no public scoring rubric exists,
so over-index on the two stated criteria (innovation, interdisciplinarity) and the presentation
stage.

## Cross-scheme strategy notes

- **Same core idea, four framings:** MSCA scores the researcher-host-project triangle (training,
  two-way transfer, career); DFG scores the project (hypotheses, work programme, data handling);
  Humboldt scores the person (record, key publications, the outline's originality — the idea must
  be yours, not the host's); GFZ scores innovation × interdisciplinarity. Do not paste one text
  into another template.
- **Timeline arithmetic (as of July 2026):** GFZ Discovery closes 13 Sep 2026; MSCA-PF ~9 Sep 2026;
  Humboldt next committee windows Jul/Nov (submit 4–7 months ahead; rounds cap at 800
  applications); DFG is rolling with ~6–9 months to decision. MSCA and GFZ deadlines collide in
  the same week — sequence drafting accordingly.
- **Eligibility traps to check first:** MSCA ≤12 months in Germany in last 36; Humboldt ≤90 days
  in Germany in last 18 months and PhD ≤4 years; GFZ PhD <6 years + internal nomination; DFG at
  a Helmholtz host needs the university-cooperation arrangement or the within-6-years + 45%
  exception.

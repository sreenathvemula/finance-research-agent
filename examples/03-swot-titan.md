# Example: evidence-cited SWOT — Titan Company

**Tools used:** `business_profile` + `competitive_position` (VRIO-tested moat), `financial_health`
(internal strengths/weaknesses), `valuation_summary` (the price risk), `shareholding_trends`
(governance). Strengths are tested against **VRIO**; externals framed with **Porter / PESTEL** —
claimed moats aren't taken at face value.

> Snapshots from a local data lake; illustrative, not investment advice.

## The moat, quantified (`competitive_position`)

![Titan vs jewellery peers](../docs/img/titan_vs_peers.png)

| 5-yr average | Titan | Kalyan | Senco |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| ROE | **25.9%** | 9.6% | 13.2% |
| ROCE | **31.1%** | 19.0% | 21.4% |
| Net margin | **6.5%** | 2.2% | 3.2% |
| Interest coverage | **10.4×** | 2.4× | 2.8× |

3,603 stores (Tanishq 561, Titan Eye 839, CaratLane 370); same-store sales growth **14–28%**
across formats. This is a *Valuable, Rare, Organised* advantage — trust + scale in a
trust-scarce category. Only "Inimitable" is partial: rivals can (and do) open stores, but not
replicate the Tanishq brand quickly.

## SWOT

| Strengths (internal, VRIO-tested) | Weaknesses (internal) |
|---|---|
| Category-leading brand & 3,600-store scale; peers earn a *third* of its ROE | **Cash conversion: cumulative CFO only 0.62× PAT** — jewellery working-capital drain (`financial_health` 🟥) |
| 25.9% ROE / 31.1% ROCE, 5yr PAT CAGR ~28% | Net debt ₹335cr → ₹15,022cr (FY19→FY25); borrowings 100× in a decade |
| Zero promoter pledge; Tata parentage | **92% of revenue is Jewellery** — single-segment concentration |
| Pricing power: ad spend only 2.2% of sales | Watches/Eyewear/Others still sub-scale (~8% combined) |

| Opportunities (external, Porter/PESTEL) | Threats (external) |
|---|---|
| Formalisation: organised jewellery still a minority of a huge market | Kalyan/Senco expanding aggressively; new large-cap entrants eyeing the category |
| CaratLane / digital + younger brands (Mia, Zoya) | **Gold-price & import-duty shocks** hit demand and working capital (PESTEL: economic/political) |
| International (currently ~4% of revenue) | Regulatory: hallmarking, PMLA/KYC on high-value cash purchases |

## The catch the SWOT surfaces

The business quality is not in question — the **price** is. At **P/E ~72–82 and P/B ~24**
(`valuation_summary`), the stock prices the moat *and* flawless execution, while the 0.62× cash
conversion says the reported profit isn't fully turning into cash. **Strong company, demanding
valuation, one working-capital flag to watch** — the agent lays out all three and leaves the
call to you.
